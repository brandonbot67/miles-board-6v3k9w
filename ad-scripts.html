1. Pattern references — 8 that actually fit local home service

Each one: what the pattern is, the 3s hook, problem, proof, CTA, and why it fits you.

P1 — The 3-Second Rule / 3-3-3 structure

Structure: Hook (0-3s) → Value (4-15s) → Story/payoff (16-45s) → CTA (last 5s).
Why it matters: viewers decide inside 3 seconds. 65% who pass 3s reach 10s; 45% of those reach 30s. So the entire fight is the first sentence.
Applies to you: your first spoken words must be the question. No "Hey guys, Brandon here." That's a 2-second tax you cannot afford.
Source: Real Marketing Solutions — Best Structure for Short Form Videos, Cloudix — The 3-Second Hook Rule for 2026

P2 — The problem-question hook

Pattern: open on the audience's pain phrased as a yes/no question. "Still getting rejected for mortgages?" The viewer answers in their head, and that internal "yes" is the commitment.
Hook (0-3s): a question they'd answer yes to.
Applies to you: this is exactly your instinct. "How many pool companies have you called that never called you back?" is the homeowner version.
Source: Real Marketing Solutions, Sovran — Video Ad Hook Examples

P3 — Geo-callout hook ("Attention [town] homeowners")

Pattern: name the city in the first line. "Houston homeowners — ready for your dream backyard?" Instantly filters and instantly relevant.
Problem/proof/CTA: local pain → local proof → local phone.
Applies to you: "Main Line" and "Hockessin" do more qualifying work than any targeting setting. Use as a text overlay in the first frame even when you don't say it out loud — it filters silently while your spoken hook does the emotional work.
Source: Hook Agency — Home Services Facebook Ads Guide, gethookd — Plumber Facebook Ads

P4 — Ad copy as a two-sided filter (qualify-in-the-ad)

Pattern: the ad should repel the wrong lead as hard as it attracts the right one. Add explicit qualifiers — service area, price range, customer type, commitment-level CTA — so bad fits self-select out before the click. "Every unqualified click costs you twice: once for the click, once for the follow-up."
Applies to you: this is the whole thesis of your hook. Say the route out loud. Say "I'm not for everybody." That sentence is not a liability, it's the filter.
Source: ClicksGeek — Stop Unqualified Leads From Advertising, ClicksGeek — Poor Quality Leads From Ads, WordStream — 6 Ways to Increase Lead Quality

P5 — Qualifying question inside the lead form

Pattern: Meta lead forms with a custom multiple-choice question filter junk before it reaches your phone, and capture context you'd otherwise burn a call getting.
Applies to you: if you use a lead form, the question is "What kind of pool?" (Inground / Above ground / Not sure) and "Your town?" as a dropdown of your 22 areas. That's your route filter, automated.
Source: Graphed — Facebook Ads for Pool Cleaners 2026, EZ Pool Biller — Targeting Homeowners with Pools

P6 — Founder talking-head / UGC over polished production

Pattern: a real owner's face beats a produced spot. Reported gaps: ~4x CTR vs branded creative at ~50% lower CPC; UGC-style consistently outperforming polished professional content on conversion, CPM and ROAS. Treat the exact multiples as directional vendor-reported numbers, not gospel — the direction is well-established, the decimals are marketing.
Applies to you: you already recorded black-background talking heads. That's the right instinct. Don't upgrade the production. Upgrade the first sentence.
Source: Thinkster — Why Real Faces Beat Polished Creatives, Expanse Digital — Why UGC Ads Outperform Professional Ads, Marpipe — Do UGC Ads Still Work?

P7 — Process proof / green-to-clean demonstration

Pattern: show the work, not the claim. Ugly before → process → clean after → CTA card. A 30s video of a tech actually maintaining a pool builds trust faster than any static image.
Applies to you: already your house style (SOCIAL_AD_KIT.md Format 1). The contrast shot at the shitty above-ground is this pattern's hook frame.
Source: Slamdot — 7 Must-Haves For High-Converting Pool Service Facebook Ads, Waterline Growth — Video Ads for Pool Builders

P8 — Match the CTA to the intent, and answer fast

Pattern: phone calls convert far above web forms in home services (reported ~46% call conversion vs ~1.7% form average — different-intent populations, not a like-for-like test, so read it as "calls are higher-intent," not "forms are broken"). But the modern path is call plus text plus book. Speed is the real variable: 78% go with whoever responds first; sub-60-second response is where the lift is.
Applies to you: text-first is your edge. It's lower friction than a call, higher intent than a form, and it's where "text your town + pool size" self-qualifies in one message. Your whole brand promise is "the dude who texts back" — the CTA should be the promise.
Source: PCN — 2026 Calls vs Forms Lead Study, Supply House Times — Home services call performance report, Click-Vision — Home Services Marketing Statistics 2026

2. Pattern card — PCD ad rules

Qualify in the ad

Say the filter out loud. It is the ad.

  • Route: "Main Line and Northern Delaware." Name 2-3 towns.
  • Fit: "I'm not for everybody. I run a route." — this line is the qualifier.
  • Effort: "Text me your town and pool size" is a 5-second ask that a tire-kicker won't bother with and a real customer does instantly.
  • Honesty as filter: "If you're outside it I'll tell you straight" converts the rejection into a trust signal.

Social proof without BS

Only verified fact you have: 32 customers in about 4 months, first year, in your area, confirmable. That is a genuinely strong number. Say it plainly and it does more than any invented superlative.

  • ✅ "Started last year. Four months in, 32 customers."
  • ✅ "Ask around — people will tell you."
  • ✅ "No contracts." (true, core to brand)
  • ✅ "I'm a Certified Pool Operator." (true — but only if you want the credential framing; it's slightly off-voice for PCD)
  • ❌ "#1 pool company" / "award-winning" / "10 years" / "hundreds of happy customers" / "satisfaction guaranteed" / "best in the Main Line"
  • ❌ Any customer count other than 32. Any timeframe other than ~4 months. Don't round up to "almost a year" — "not even a year" is better copy anyway.
  • ❌ Fake testimonials, actors playing customers, or reviews you haven't got.

Offer / CTA

  • Primary: Text 302-496-6367 — town + pool size.
  • Secondary: Call the same number.
  • Tertiary: poolcleaningdude.com/contact-us (Get Quote).
  • Never stack all three in a spoken CTA. One spoken, the others on screen.

What NOT to say — hard rules

  1. Never mention Tri-State Aquatic Solutions. TSAS is never public in PCD creative. PCD voice only.
  2. No invented guarantees. No money-back, no "satisfaction guaranteed," no response-time promise you haven't tested.
  3. No cover advice, no chemical advice, no DIY tips. (Liability + you can't know their setup.)
  4. No assuming pool type. If the ad targets both, the words must work for a mesh-safety-cover inground and a solid-cover above-ground.
  5. No shaming the gross pool. Make it fixable, not embarrassing.
  6. No specific dollar figures in video unless you re-check src/app/api/chat/route.ts first. The old $65/visit copy is dead.
  7. No corporate voice. "Elevated aquatic care" is a different company.

6. Film guide — today

Shot list

Location 1 — Beautiful inground (PRIMARY, most of your time here)

  1. H1 full 25s, medium shot, waist-up, pool + water visible behind your shoulder. 3 takes.
  2. H1 again, wider — you smaller, pool bigger. 2 takes. (This is your B-variant background.)
  3. B-roll, no talking, 10s each: water surface with light on it; skimmer basket; you walking the deck with a pole; clean water close-up.
  4. Silent end-card plate: 5s of just the pool, no you — you'll burn the phone number over it.

Location 2 — Decent inground

  1. H2 full 15s. 3 takes.
  2. H1 alt take here too if time — same words, different background. That's a clean A/B: identical script, background is the only variable.
  3. B-roll: vacuum head, brushing the wall, equipment pad running.

Location 3 — Above-ground / shitty pool (CONTRAST)

  1. H3 hook, 8-12s. 3 takes.
  2. Cold open plate: 3s of the bad water with NO you in frame. This is a hook frame — it opens the ad before you speak.
  3. Contrast pair: same framing, bad pool → then cut to Location 1's clean water. Two shots, one point.

⚠️ Above-ground caution: the contrast shot has to read as "this is fixable", not "look at this idiot's pool." Don't shoot anything identifiable — no house, no fence, no address. And don't let the framing imply above-ground owners are the problem; they're a real chunk of your market and they're price-sensitive, not stupid.

How to use the black-background takes you already have

Don't waste them and don't lead with them.

  • Use as Variant A (control). They're the cleanest test of the words alone — no background doing any work. Whatever background beats black, beats it on merit.
  • Use as the mid-section. Hook at the pool → cut to black-background for the 32-customers proof → back to pool for the CTA. The cut to black is a pattern interrupt exactly where attention usually dies (8-14s), and it makes the proof land like you're being serious with them.
  • Don't use as the 0-3s hook. Black background gives the scroll-stopper nothing to grab.
  • Check: if the audio on the black takes doesn't match today's audio, don't intercut them. Mismatched room tone reads as fake faster than anything else.
